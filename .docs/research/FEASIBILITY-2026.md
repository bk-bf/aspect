# ASPECT Feasibility Analysis (2026)

**Created:** 2026-01-31  
**Updated:** 2026-02-15  
**Status:** Reference — findings incorporated into [DECISIONS.md](../DECISIONS.md) and [ROADMAP.md](../features/open/ROADMAP.md)

---

## Executive Summary

Robotic lunar hydrogen extraction is conditionally feasible with a realistic 8-year
timeline (2026-2033), provided scope expands beyond excavation-only to address the
complete hydrogen production value chain.

**Verdict:** Conditionally Feasible

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Technical feasibility | Technology exists; water electrolysis at TRL-5 (0.9 kg/hr O₂, 0.12 kg/hr H₂) |
| Funding pathways | NASA SBIR/STTR Phase I ($50-150k); ESA BICs; commercial partnerships |
| Market opportunity | Expanding (30,000+ space jobs 2023-2025); not yet at "first mover" stage |
| Execution risk | Requires 7-8 year sustained commitment, $2-5M beyond hobby phase |
| Capability gap | Formal credentials, industry experience, or established partnerships required |

---

## Industry Landscape (2026)

- **NASA ISRU status:** Water/volatile extraction lacks sufficient resource knowledge to
  proceed without significant risk; no resource data for first-mover advantage yet
- **Competitor TRL:** Blue Origin and ispace at TRL-4-5 for integrated systems
- **Technology:** Water electrolysis TRL-5 commercially available (OxEon et al.)
- **European effort:** LUWEX project advancing extraction from TRL-2/3 → TRL-4,
  processing kg-scale icy regolith
- **Market timing:** Entering in 2029 with stronger credentials may provide better
  positioning than forcing execution in 2026

---

## Scope: Why Excavation-Only Is Insufficient

A system focused solely on regolith excavation does not address the complete hydrogen
production value chain:

1. Water extraction from icy regolith
2. Purification and capture
3. Electrolysis for H₂/O₂ production
4. Hydrogen storage and handling
5. Thermal management in lunar environment
6. Power systems for continuous operation

**Adopted approach:** Modular end-to-end demonstration — excavation → water extraction
→ electrolysis → hydrogen storage — partnering with commercial electrolysis providers
rather than building in-house. See [ROADMAP.md](../features/open/ROADMAP.md) Phase 3.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Compress sim-only phase** — hardware prototyping starts in parallel (Phase 2),
   not after simulation is "complete" (see D-010 in [DECISIONS.md](../DECISIONS.md))
2. **Partner on electrolysis** — do not build in-house; integrate proven TRL-5+ stacks
3. **Align with NASA resource prospecting** — reduce water ice location uncertainty
   before committing to extraction site design
4. **Scale progression:** 1:10 (2026) → 1:5 (2027) → 1:1 subsystems (2029, requires
   funding) → flight-qualified (2031, requires NASA/commercial contracts)
